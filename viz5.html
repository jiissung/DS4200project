Investigating Socio-Economic Situations

This heatmap shows how socio-economic status factors can influence exam scores. This reveals a clear upward trend across both dimensions, family income, and access to resources. Students with higher family income and greater access to resources consistently achieve the highest average exam scores, while those with lower income and limited resources tend to have lower performance. The gradient pattern from the bottom-left (low income, low resources) to the top-right (high income, high resources) highlights how these factors reinforce each other. One can see that even when holding a factor constant, there is still an improvement in scores. The visualization emphasizes that academic performance is strongly shaped by both economic background and access to supportive resources

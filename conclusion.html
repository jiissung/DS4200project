Conclusion

Education plays a foundational role in individual growth, social mobility, and societal duties. Student performance in education is attributed to a multitude of academic, social, economic, and personal factors. It is important to understand how these factors interact and to work towards an educational system that benefits educational success. This project seeks to provide a better understanding of these factors by visualizing meaningful relationships. By examining these relationships, the study aims to provide insights that can provide potential solutions to improve educational outcomes.

References

Suleiman, I.B., Okunade, O.A., Dada, E.G. et al. Key factors influencing students’ academic performance. Journal of Electrical Systems and Inf Technol 11, 41 (2024). https://doi.org/10.1186/s43067-024-00166-w

This study examines the socioeconomic, educational, individual, and behavioral factors that influence a student’s performance by using statistical analysis. Collectively, the authors identified study habits, prior achievements, and engagement, to name a few, as significant predictors of a student’s success. The findings emphasized that academic success was shaped by multiple interacting variables over a single important variable.

Beckham, N.R., Akeh, L.J., Pratama Mitaart, G.N., Moniaga, J.V. Determining factors that affect student performance using various machine learning methods. Procedia Computer Science 216, 597–603 (2023). https://doi.org/10.1016/j.procs.2022.12.174s

This paper breaks down factors affecting student grades using Pearson correlation and machine learning models such as random forest and decision tree algorithms. The results display that past academic failures negatively impacted performance, while parental education correlated with a positive influence on a student. The findings of the study show that combining statistical analysis with predictive modeling can be used to identify key determinants of academic success.

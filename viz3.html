Impact of Categorical Variables on Exam Scores

This visualization demonstrates that attendance has the strongest influence on exam scores among the selected categorical factors. Students with higher attendance levels consistently achieve higher scores, indicating that regular participation and engagement in class play a critical role in academic success. While other factors,such as parental involvement, access to resources, and motivation level,do show some variation in exam performance, their effects are comparatively smaller and less consistent. The distributions across these categories tend to overlap more, suggesting that their impact is present but not as dominant.

Analyzing Contributors to Youth Education

Justin Jisung Lee | Dan Kim | Sean Kim

Introduction

Education plays a foundational role in individual growth, social mobility, and societal duties. Student performance in education is attributed to a multitude of academic, social, economic, and personal factors. It is important to understand how these factors interact and to work towards an educational system that benefits educational success. This project seeks to provide a better understanding of these factors by visualizing meaningful relationships. By examining these relationships, the study aims to provide insights that can provide potential solutions to improve educational outcomes.

Data

This project utilizes data from Kaggle.com. The dataset collects information about student performance factors. The dataset contains 6607 observations and 20 features. The features are: hours studied, attendance, parental involvement, access to resources, extracurricular activities, hours slept, previous exam scores, motivation level, internet access, tutoring sessions, family income, teacher quality, school type, peer influence, physical activity, learning disabilities, parental education level, distance from home, gender, and exam scores.

Methodology

This project highlights the factors that influence student performance through three interactive visualizations. Each visualization examines different relationships in the data, helping users clearly identify the variables most strongly associated with academic success.

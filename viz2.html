Investigating Average Exam Scores on Factors

Across all selected factors, students in the “high” category consistently achieve higher exam scores, indicating a positive relationship between these factors and academic performance. Whether it is higher levels of parental involvement, greater access to resources, or increased participation in extracurricular activities, being in the top category is generally associated with improved outcomes. This pattern suggests that greater support, engagement, and access tend to reinforce student success.
